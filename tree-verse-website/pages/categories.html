Herbs : Small, soft, green, and tender plants without woody stems.

Annual herbs : Complete life cycle in one season.

Biennial herbs : Take two seasons to complete life cycle.

Perennial herbs : Live for more than two years.

Medicinal herbs : Used in traditional and modern medicine.

Culinary herbs : Used for cooking and flavor.

Shrubs : Medium-sized plants with woody stems and branches near the ground.

Flowering shrubs : Grown for flowers.

Foliage shrubs : Grown for attractive leaves.

Medicinal shrubs : Have medicinal value.

Evergreen shrubs : Stay green all year.

Deciduous shrubs : Shed leaves seasonally.

Trees : Tall, woody plants with a single main stem or trunk.

Evergreen trees : Do not shed all leaves at once.

Deciduous trees : Shed all leaves annually.

Fruit-bearing trees : Produce fruits.

Timber trees : Used for wood.

Ornamental trees : Grown for decoration.

Creepers : Plants with weak stems that grow along the ground.

Vegetable creepers : Grow vegetables.

Medicinal creepers : Used in traditional remedies.

Ground cover creepers : Used for covering soils.

Climbers : Plants that grow upwards with support using tendrils or twining stems.

Tendril climbers : Use tendrils to climb.

Twining climbers : Use stems to wrap around support.

Hook climbers : Use hooks/thorns.

Root climbers : Use adventitious roots.

Flowering climbers : Ornamental.
